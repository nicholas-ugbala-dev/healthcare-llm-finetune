Q: How is malaria diagnosed and treated?
A: Malaria diagnosis involves clinical evaluation of symptoms along with laboratory tests to confirm its presence in blood cells or tissues. The following methods can be used for diagnosing Malaria:

1. Microscopy - Blood smears stained using Giemsa stain under microscope show characteristic ring-shaped parasites.
2. Rapid Diagnostic Tests (RDTs) - RDT kits detect antigens present inside infected red blood cell by antigen-antibody reaction which results into color change indicating positive result.

Treatment options include:
   * Artemisinin-based combination therapies like artemether-lumefantrine or dihydroartemisins combined with other antimalarial drugs such as chloroquine or mefloquine depending upon region-specific resistance patterns among Plasmodium species causing disease locally.
   * Supportive care measures including hydration management according to severity level of infection & monitoring vital signs closely during recovery phase post-treatment initiation.

Q: What are the warning signs of a heart attack?
A: Warning sign for Heart Attack include:

1) Chest pain or discomfort that may radiate to your arms and neck.
2) Pain in one arm (or both).
3) Shortness of breath with no underlying lung disease.
4) Cold sweats.
5) Nausea and vomiting.
6) Dizziness or fainting.
7) Weakness or fatigue.

Please note these symptoms can also be present during other conditions like asthma, bronchitis etc., so it is always better to consult an expert if any such symptom appears suddenly without prior history. Hope this helps! Let me know if I can assist further.
